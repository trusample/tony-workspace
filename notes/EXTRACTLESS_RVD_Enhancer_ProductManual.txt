01 INTRODUCTION
PCRopsis™ Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer is engineered to simultaneously bind a variety of reverse transcriptase quantitative polymerase chain reaction (RT-qPCR) and PCR inhibitors found in specimens, lyse cells and stabilize nucleic acids in a manner that's compatible with RT-qPCR / PCR. The product consists of a proprietary mixture of peptides, salts, stabilizers, buffers, sodium azide, and RVD Enhancer to achieve these tasks. Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer allows for nucleic acid extraction-free sample processing without centrifugations or other sample manipulations, which may introduce errors, contaminants, and/or skew the representation of RNA fragments.

06 WRITTEN PROTOCOL
Thoroughly mix Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer to ensure homogeneity, but avoid creating bubbles unnecessarily 
Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer has a hazy, white color when homogenized and normal settlement occurs if not regularly mixed 
Mix 1 volume of sample (20 μL) with 1 volume of Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer (20 μL) in a thin walled tube (0.2 ~ 0.6 mL) or 96-well PCR plate 
Pipette up & down to ensure complete mixing and then cap tube or apply plate sealer to plate to prevent evaporation 
Heat diluted sample at 95°C and let cool at room temperature for ~10 seconds before continuing 
Recommended heating times at 95°C:
Mammalian: 		5 minutes
Viruses: 		10~15 minutes
Bacteria / Fungi: 	15~20 minutes
NOTE: heating for a longer period of time does not negatively affect results
Make sure the heating device has reached the desired temperature before applying sample 
Sample heating can be performed using a controlled heating block or thermal cycler

08 TROUBLESHOOTING & SUGGESTIONS
Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer is optimized for the amplification of gene targets from specimens in compatible transport mediums and may not be applicable for other applications or mediums.
For best results, use recently collected samples in compatible transport mediums that have been stored at ~4°C since collection.
Ensure that the processed sample consist of <30% of the total PCR mixture, since high concentrations of the processed sample may inhibit PCR for some applications. 
Take care in maintaining the sterility of your Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer stock after use.
Heat Reagent RVD with RVD Enhancer / sample mixture for a few minutes longer if you observe suboptimal lysis.
It’s recommended to use the heated Reagent RVD with Enhancer + sample mixture for downstream applications within a day, although samples may be stable for months at 4°C  or -20°C.
